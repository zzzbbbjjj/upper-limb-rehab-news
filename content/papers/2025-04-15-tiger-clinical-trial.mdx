## 中文摘要

TIGER 是少数完成**正式临床试验**的绳索驱动手部康复外骨骼之一。这项单臂前瞻性干预研究纳入16名慢性脑卒中患者（平均病程21.7个月），进行为期9周、每周2次的20分钟机器人训练。结果显示：**FMA-UE 总分和子项显著改善**，Box and Blocks 测试和 Motor Activity Log 评分均显著提高。值得注意的是，基线腕/手部 FMA-UE 子评分是预测手功能恢复的最佳指标。该研究为绳索驱动外骨骼的临床有效性提供了重要证据。

## English Summary

TIGER is one of the few cable-driven hand exoskeletons to complete a **formal clinical trial**. This single-arm prospective study enrolled 16 chronic stroke patients (mean 21.7 months post-stroke) for 9 weeks of twice-weekly 20-minute robotic training. Results showed significant improvements in **FMA-UE** total and sub-scores, Box and Blocks Test, and Motor Activity Log. Notably, baseline wrist/hand FMA-UE sub-score was the best predictor of recovery. This study provides critical clinical evidence for cable-driven exoskeleton efficacy.
